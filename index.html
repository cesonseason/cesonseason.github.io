🔬 Nb₂O₅:TiO₂ Electrochromic & Battery Material 2025 | Individual
Problem: Traditional TiNb₂O₇ requires high-temperature annealing that is incompatible with glass substrates for smart window applications.
Data: Experimental data from material characterization tests, including visible light modulation rates and energy storage capacity measurements.
Approach: Developed low-temperature Nb₂O₅:TiO₂ dual-function film. Used Nb-Ti electronic coupling effect to optimize performance. Conducted material synthesis, data measurement, and results analysis.
Outcome/Impact: Achieved 40% visible light modulation and 0.8mAh/cm² energy storage at temperatures below 350°C, providing new solution for smart window industrialization.
My Contribution: Full project lifecycle - topic selection, experimental design, data measurement, results analysis, and thesis writing. Advisor: Prof. Wu Jiawei.
